## Example Case Study 1: Small Business Owner

### Title
**How Amara Saved $3,000 in 6 Months and Opened Her Dream Bakery**

### Hero Section

**Visual:** Photo of Amara in her bakery

**Quick Stats:**
- **Savings Goal:** $3,000
- **Time Frame:** 6 months
- **Group Size:** 10 members
- **Contribution:** $50/week
- **Outcome:** Business launched successfully

---

### Executive Summary

Amara Okafor, a 32-year-old aspiring entrepreneur from Lagos, Nigeria, used Soroban Ajo to save $3,000 in six months. With her Ajo group's support and the transparency of blockchain technology, she achieved her goal and opened her dream bakery.

**Key Results:**
- ✅ Saved $3,000 in 6 months
- ✅ 100% contribution rate from all members
- ✅ Successfully launched business
- ✅ Now employs 3 people

---

### Background & Challenge

**About Amara:**
Amara had dreamed of opening a bakery for years. She had the skills—she'd been baking for family and friends since she was a teenager—but lacked the capital to rent a space and buy equipment.

**The Challenge:**
- Needed $3,000 for startup costs
- No access to traditional bank loans (no collateral)
- Previous Ajo experience ended badly (coordinator disappeared with funds)
- Skeptical about online savings platforms
- Wanted to involve friends but they lived in different cities

**Quote:**
> "I tried saving on my own, but something always came up. I needed the discipline of a group, but I was scared after my last Ajo experience went wrong."

---

### Solution & Implementation

**Why Soroban Ajo:**
Amara discovered Soroban Ajo through a friend in the diaspora. The blockchain transparency appealed to her after her previous bad experience.

**How She Used It:**

**1. Created Her Group (Week 1)**
- Set contribution: $50/week
- Duration: 12 weeks
- Max members: 10
- Named it "Dream Chasers"

**2. Invited Members (Week 1-2)**
- 5 friends from Lagos
- 3 friends in London (diaspora)
- 2 family members in Abuja
- All committed to the goal

**3. Contributed Consistently (Weeks 1-12)**
- Never missed a contribution
- Received reminders via email
- Tracked progress on dashboard
- Encouraged other members

**4. Received Payout (Week 6)**
- All members contributed on time
- Received $500 (10 members × $50)
- Transaction completed in seconds
- Verified on blockchain

**5. Continued Supporting Group (Weeks 7-12)**
- Kept contributing for other members
- Group completed successfully
- All 10 members received their payouts

**Quote:**
> "The transparency was incredible. I could see every transaction on the blockchain. No one could cheat or disappear with our money."

---

### Results & Impact

**Financial Results:**
- **Total Saved:** $3,000 ($500 from payout + $2,500 from continued contributions)
- **Time Frame:** 6 months (2 complete Ajo cycles)
- **Success Rate:** 100% (all members received payouts)
- **Additional Benefit:** Built savings discipline

**Business Impact:**
- Rented bakery space in Ikeja
- Purchased commercial oven and equipment
- Launched "Amara's Delights" bakery
- First month revenue: $2,000
- Now employs 3 people

**Personal Impact:**
- Achieved lifelong dream
- Financial independence
- Community respect
- Inspired others

**Quote:**
> "Soroban Ajo didn't just help me save money—it changed my life. I'm now a business owner, and I'm helping others achieve their dreams too."

---

### Testimonial

**Full Testimonial:**

> "I was skeptical at first. After my previous Ajo experience, I didn't trust anyone with my money. But Soroban Ajo was different. The blockchain transparency meant I could verify every transaction. I could see when members contributed, when payouts happened, everything.
>
> The platform made it so easy. I created my group in minutes, invited friends from different cities, and we all saved together. The reminders kept us accountable, and the automatic payouts meant no one had to chase anyone for money.
>
> In six months, I saved $3,000 and opened my bakery. I'm now a business owner, employing three people, and making a living doing what I love. None of this would have been possible without Soroban Ajo.
>
> I've already started two more Ajo groups—one for equipment upgrades and one for my daughter's education. I tell everyone about it. If you have a dream and need to save, this is the way to do it."

**— Amara Okafor, Owner, Amara's Delights Bakery, Lagos, Nigeria**

---

### Key Takeaways

**For Users:**
1. **Start with a clear goal** - Amara knew exactly what she needed and why
2. **Choose committed members** - Her group had 100% participation
3. **Stay consistent** - Never missed a contribution
4. **Use transparency** - Blockchain verification built trust
5. **Think long-term** - Started additional groups for future goals

**For Soroban Ajo:**
1. Transparency builds trust (especially for users with bad experiences)
2. Cross-border groups work well (diaspora + local)
3. Clear goals drive commitment
4. Email reminders improve participation
5. Success stories inspire others

---

### What's Next for Amara

- Expanding bakery to second location
- Training program for young bakers
- Using Ajo for business expansion capital
- Helping others start their own businesses
- Ambassador for Soroban Ajo in Lagos

## Example Case Study 2: Diaspora Family

### Title
**How the Adeyemi Family Stayed Connected and Saved $10,000 Across 3 Continents**

### Hero Section

**Visual:** Family photo with members in different locations

**Quick Stats:**
- **Family Members:** 15 across UK, US, Nigeria
- **Savings Goal:** $10,000 for family reunion
- **Time Frame:** 12 months
- **Contribution:** $100/month per member
- **Outcome:** Epic family reunion in Lagos

---

### Executive Summary

The Adeyemi family, spread across London, New York, and Lagos, used Soroban Ajo to save together for a family reunion. Despite time zones and currencies, they successfully saved $10,000 and hosted their first full family gathering in 10 years.

**Key Results:**
- ✅ 15 family members participated
- ✅ Saved $10,000 in 12 months
- ✅ 98% contribution rate
- ✅ Strengthened family bonds
- ✅ Successful reunion with 50+ attendees

---

### Background & Challenge

**About the Adeyemis:**
The Adeyemi family has members in three countries. The parents are in Lagos, children and grandchildren are spread across London and New York. They wanted to have a big family reunion but coordinating savings across borders was challenging.

**The Challenge:**
- Family members in different countries and time zones
- Different currencies (Naira, Pounds, Dollars)
- Previous attempts at coordinating savings failed
- No trust in traditional money transfer services (high fees)
- Wanted transparency so everyone could see contributions
- Needed a way to hold everyone accountable

**Quote:**
> "We tried WhatsApp groups and bank transfers, but it was chaos. People forgot, fees were high, and we couldn't track who had paid. We needed something better." — Tunde Adeyemi, London

---

### Solution & Implementation

**Why Soroban Ajo:**
Tunde, the eldest son in London, discovered Soroban Ajo and proposed it to the family. The ability to use different tokens (XLM, USDC) and the transparency appealed to everyone.

**How They Used It:**

**1. Family Meeting (Virtual)**
- Discussed reunion goals
- Agreed on $10,000 budget
- Decided on $100/month per adult member
- 15 adults committed

**2. Group Setup**
- Created "Adeyemi Family Reunion 2026"
- Contribution: $100/month (in USDC for consistency)
- Duration: 12 months
- All members joined within a week

**3. Monthly Contributions**
- Automated reminders
- Members contributed in their local currency
- Converted to USDC on Stellar
- Minimal fees (<$0.10 per transaction)

**4. Rotating Payouts**
- Used payouts for reunion expenses
- Venue booking
- Catering
- Decorations
- Travel assistance for some members

**5. Successful Reunion**
- 50+ family members attended
- 3-day celebration in Lagos
- Memories for a lifetime

**Quote:**
> "Soroban Ajo made it so easy. Everyone could see who had contributed, and the blockchain transparency meant no one could cheat. It brought us together." — Folake Adeyemi, New York

---

### Results & Impact

**Financial Results:**
- **Total Saved:** $10,000
- **Average Fee per Transaction:** $0.08
- **Total Fees:** $14.40 (vs. $500+ with traditional remittance)
- **Savings:** $485.60 on fees alone

**Family Impact:**
- First full family gathering in 10 years
- 50+ family members attended
- Strengthened family bonds
- Created lasting memories
- Inspired other families

**Ongoing Impact:**
- Started annual reunion Ajo group
- Created education fund Ajo for grandchildren
- Other families in community adopted the model

**Quote:**
> "This wasn't just about saving money. It was about bringing our family together. Soroban Ajo made that possible." — Chief Adeyemi, Lagos

---

### Testimonial

**Full Testimonial:**

> "As a family spread across three continents, staying connected is hard. We tried everything—WhatsApp groups, bank transfers, money transfer services—but nothing worked well. Fees were high, tracking was impossible, and people forgot.
>
> Soroban Ajo changed everything. We created our family group, and everyone could contribute in their own currency. The platform converted everything to USDC, so there was no confusion. The transparency meant everyone could see who had contributed, and the reminders kept us accountable.
>
> In 12 months, we saved $10,000 and hosted the most amazing family reunion. Seeing my parents surrounded by all their children and grandchildren was priceless. We're now using Ajo for other family goals—education, emergencies, annual reunions.
>
> If your family is spread out and you want to save together, this is the answer. It's not just a savings tool—it's a family connector."

**— Tunde Adeyemi, London, UK**

---

### Key Takeaways

**For Users:**
1. **Cross-border groups work** - Different countries, one platform
2. **Use stablecoins** - USDC eliminated currency confusion
3. **Transparency builds accountability** - Everyone could see contributions
4. **Low fees matter** - Saved $485 vs. traditional methods
5. **Beyond money** - Strengthened family relationships

**For Soroban Ajo:**
1. Diaspora is a key market
2. Multi-currency support is essential
3. Transparency drives participation
4. Low fees are a major selling point
5. Emotional benefits (connection) matter as much as financial

- Education fund for grandchildren

- Family ambassadors for Soroban Ajo

## Case Study Development Process

### 1. Identify Candidates

**Criteria:**
- Achieved significant goal
- Compelling story
- Willing to share publicly
- Diverse backgrounds
- Different use cases

**Sources:**
- User surveys
- Support tickets
- Social media mentions
- Referral program
- Community forum

### 2. Initial Outreach

**Email Template:**

```
Subject: Share Your Soroban Ajo Success Story

Hi [Name],

We noticed that you [achieved goal] using Soroban Ajo. Congratulations!

We'd love to feature your story to inspire others in our community. Your experience could help someone else achieve their dreams.

Would you be interested in participating in a case study? It would involve:
- 30-minute interview (phone/video)
- Sharing your story and results
- Optional photo
- Review and approval before publishing

As a thank you, we'll:
- Feature you prominently on our website
- Share your story across our social media
- Provide [incentive: $100 credit, exclusive swag, etc.]

Interested? Reply to this email or schedule a time here: [Calendar link]

Best,
[Your Name]
Soroban Ajo Team
```

### 3. Interview

**Questions to Ask:**

**Background:**
- Tell me about yourself
- What was your situation before Soroban Ajo?
- What goal were you trying to achieve?
- What challenges were you facing?

**Discovery:**
- How did you hear about Soroban Ajo?
- What made you decide to try it?
- Were you skeptical? Why or why not?

**Experience:**
- Walk me through how you used the platform
- How did you set up your group?
- How did you invite members?
- What was the contribution process like?
- Any challenges or issues?

**Results:**
- What did you achieve?
- How long did it take?
- What was the impact (financial, personal, etc.)?
- How did it change your life?

**Reflection:**
- What surprised you most?
- What would you tell someone considering Soroban Ajo?
- What are your future plans?
- Would you recommend it? Why?

### 4. Write Case Study

**Process:**
1. Transcribe interview
2. Extract key quotes
3. Organize into template structure
4. Write narrative
5. Add data and metrics
6. Create visuals

**Writing Tips:**
- Use their voice and words
- Tell a story, not just facts
- Include specific details
- Show transformation
- Make it relatable
- End with inspiration

### 5. Review & Approval

**Send to Subject:**
- Full draft for review
- Request corrections
- Get photo approval
- Obtain written consent
- Confirm publication details

**Legal:**
- Signed release form
- Permission to use name/photo
- Right to edit for clarity
- Right to use in marketing

### 6. Publish & Promote

**Publication:**
- Dedicated case study page
- Blog post announcement
- Email to subscribers
- Social media posts
- Press release (if significant)

**Promotion:**
- Share across all channels
- Tag the subject (with permission)
- Create quote graphics
- Include in pitch deck
- Use in sales materials

### 7. Follow-Up

**Stay Connected:**
- Thank them publicly
- Send them the published piece
- Monitor comments and engagement
- Check in periodically
- Feature in future content
